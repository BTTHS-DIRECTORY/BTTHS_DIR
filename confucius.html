Confucius

Philosopher & Teacher

"Do not do to others what you do not want done to yourself."

Confucius (551–479 BCE) was a Chinese philosopher and politician of the Spring and Autumn period. His philosophy, known as Confucianism, emphasized personal and governmental morality, correctness of social relationships, justice, kindness, and sincerity.

He championed strong family loyalty, ancestor veneration, and respect of elders by their children and of husbands by their wives. He recommended family as a basis for ideal government.

His teachings were compiled by his disciples in the Analects and have deeply influenced Chinese, Korean, Japanese, and Vietnamese thought and life for over two thousand years.
